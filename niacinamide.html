Niacinamide

This is also known as the vitamin B3, is a water-soluble vitamin that is commonly used in skincare products. It offers multiple benefits, including improving skin texture, reducing the appearance of pores, regulating oil production, and minimizing fine lines and wrinkles.

It is known to treat acne by reducing inflammation. It also helps fade dark-spots and strengthen the skin by promoting the growth of the ceramide barrier.

Usage

People use niacinamide to prevent vitamin B3 deficiency and related conditions such as pellagra. It is also used for acne, diabetes, cancer, osteoarthritis, aging skin, skin discoloration, and many other conditions.

Storage

Store niacinamide in a cool, dry location, away from direct sunlight and heat, as exposure to these elements can degrade its potency. Check the expiration date on the product, as niacinamide typically has a shelf life of 6-12 months, depending on the formulation. Always keep the container tightly sealed to prevent exposure to air, which can cause oxidation and reduce its effectiveness over time.
