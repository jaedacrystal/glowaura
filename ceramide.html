Ceramides

These are like the natural glue that holds our skin cells together, keeping our skin smooth and hydrated. They act as a protective barrier, locking in moisture and shielding us from things like pollution and irritants. When our skin doesn’t have enough ceramides, it can become dry, itchy, and more prone to irritation.

They are known for their ability to hold water and thus are a great ingredient for dry skin. By bolstering the skin ceramides act as a barrier against irritating ingredients. This can help with inflammation as well.

Usage

They create a barrier that helps prevent moisture loss — keeping skin hydrated, plump, and supple. The barrier also protects the skin from harmful irritants, bacteria, and other environmental toxins.

Storage

Store your ceramide creams and serums in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and humidity, which can break down the ingredients. Always make sure the container is tightly closed after use to prevent air from getting in, which can degrade the product. Don’t leave your products in places with fluctuating temperatures, like a bathroom or a car, as heat can cause the formula to break down faster.
